About Brahmasri

Brahmasri Indrakanti Venkata Gopalakrishna is a highly respected Vedic Scholar, Astrologer, and Spiritual Guide based in Hyderabad, Telangana. He is the founder of the renowned center Vagdevi Jyotishalayam. Inheriting a rich spiritual lineage as the son of Surya Narayana Murthy, he has dedicated his life to studying and practicing the sacred Veda Shastras.

✦ Traditional Vedic Lineage

Having completed rigorous training in Krishnayajurveda Smartham in 2005 under the Sri Uppuluri Ganapathi Sastri Veda Sastra Parishat, Kakinada, he furthered his academic pursuits with advanced university degrees. He holds a Master of Arts (Acharya M.A.) in Phalitha Jyotisha (Astrology) with First Division honors from Rashtriya Sanskrit Vidyapeetha Deemed University, Tirupati, as well as an M.A. in Telugu and Telugu Astrology from Potti Sreeramulu Telugu University.

✦ Accolades & Modern Synthesis

His deep understanding of astrology and spiritual discourses earned him the title of "Pravachana Visharada" from the cultural associations and an **American Honorary Doctorate (Hon.D.A.)** in Astrology from the International Astrology Federation (IAF), USA, in 2022. Whether rendering Vastu consultations or guiding seekers through astrological consultations, he seamlessly bridges traditional ancient wisdom with modern applications.

About Sri MahaLakshmi Temple
(Sri Sringeri Sankaramatam)

🪷

Construction & Consecration

In an extraordinary act of devotion and selfless service, Brahmasri Indrakanti Venkata Gopalakrishna personally undertook the construction of a Mahalakshmi Temple — fulfilling a sacred calling rooted in his deep reverence for the Divine Mother. The temple, built with great devotion, stands as a living testament to his commitment to Dharma and community welfare.

🕉️

Handed Over to Sringeri Peetham

In a moment of profound spiritual significance, Brahmasri Indrakanti Venkata Gopalakrishna formally handed over the Mahalakshmi Temple to the renowned Sringeri Sharada Peetham — one of the four sacred Mathas established by Adi Shankaracharya. In recognition of this supreme act of service and his deep scholarly qualifications, the Sringeri Peetham honoured him with the prestigious title of Dharmaadhikari — the Guardian of Dharma.
